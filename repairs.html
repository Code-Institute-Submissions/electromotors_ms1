Commutator Maintenance

    Ideally, the armature is positioned correctly in a lathe and the surface of the commutator is skimmed just enough to remove all pitting, and to ensure that the surface is level. If a lathe is not available, running repairs CAN be made using progressively finer sandpaper.

Undercutting the slots in the commutator is a skilled job, but also vital. If it is not carried out correctly, there may be some of the mica left sitting above the slots which can cause commutation problems. It is also wise to be careful not to scratch the copper to avoid having to polish it again
    The final stage in the reconditioning of the commutator is to ensure that the slots are clean. Any residue of copper remaining will cause a short-circuit that could end up being worse than the ORIGINAL problem!

Bearing Replacements
Return to Repair Menu

Modern Electric Motors of a reputable quality do not, in general, fail without unusual external problems causing them to do so.

The one exception to this adage is where bearings are concerned.

One of the few maintenance tasks regularly needed to be carried out on modern equipment is the timely replacement of bearings.

We undertake this repair more often than most – in fact we recommend that bearings are ALWAYS changed when you have gone to the trouble of taking the motor out of service in the first place – and we ALWAYS insist on fitting FAG, SKF or similar.

Electromotors carries a substantial stock of bearings that allows us to undertake fast turnarounds to get you back into production very quickly.
